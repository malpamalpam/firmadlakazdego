When Is It Worth Resuming Your Business and How to Do It?

FDK Team · 12.11.2025

When to resume your business

Suspending a business is a popular solution for entrepreneurs who temporarily generate no revenue. However, the decision to resume requires careful thought. It is worth doing when new orders or contracts appear, the market situation changes favorably, or the period during which suspension was beneficial ends.

Remember that business suspension can last indefinitely — there is no longer a requirement to resume after 24 months. However, too long a suspension may raise doubts among contractors and financial institutions when assessing credibility.

Resumption procedure step by step

Resuming a business requires submitting a CEIDG-1 application with the resumption field checked. The application can be submitted electronically through biznes.gov.pl, in person at the commune office, or by registered mail. The resumption date must be specified — it can be a future or current date, but not retroactive.

After submission, CEIDG automatically notifies ZUS, the tax office, and GUS. The process is free and usually takes 1 business day.

ZUS obligations after resumption

From the day of resumption, the entrepreneur is again subject to mandatory social and health insurance. ZUS automatically restores the registration. If the entrepreneur was using the startup relief or preferential contributions before suspension, the unused period "unfreezes" and can be continued.

The first ZUS contribution after resumption must be paid by the 20th of the month following the resumption month. If resumption occurred mid-month, contributions are calculated proportionally.

Tax settlements after resumption

After resumption, the taxation form remains the same as before suspension — unless the entrepreneur submits a change request by the 20th of the month following resumption. The entrepreneur must resume keeping the Revenue and Expense Book (KPiR) or revenue records from the day of resumption.

Alternatives — incubator instead of resumption

If you plan to resume earning activity but fear the fixed costs of running your own business (especially ZUS contributions), consider a business incubator as an alternative. Within the FDK incubator, you don't pay ZUS, don't have to keep accounts, and the effective tax rate is just 6%. This solution is particularly beneficial for people unsure whether their business will generate stable revenues. The Firma Dla Każdego Foundation offers support at every stage.
