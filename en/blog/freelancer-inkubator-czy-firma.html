Three freelancer work models

The freelancer market in Poland is growing dynamically — according to the latest research, 67.9% of people working in the freelance model are under 35 years old. This is a generation that values flexibility, remote work, and independence from a single employer. At the same time, they face a key question: in what legal form should they run their business?

In 2026, a freelancer in Poland has three basic models to choose from: unregistered activity (for small revenues), a business incubator (for those who don't want to start their own company), and a sole proprietorship (JDG). Each of these models has its advantages and limitations, and the choice depends on the scale of activity, type of clients, and individual preferences.

It is worth emphasizing that there is no single universal solution. A programmer earning PLN 15,000 per month on B2B contracts will have different needs than a graphic designer doing occasional jobs for PLN 2,000. The key is understanding the costs, obligations, and possibilities of each model.

Unregistered activity

Unregistered activity is the simplest form of earning, intended for people with small revenues. In 2026, the monthly limit is 75% of the minimum wage, which at a minimum wage of PLN 4,666 gross gives approximately PLN 3,500 per month. Exceeding this limit in any month means an obligation to register a business.

The advantage of unregistered activity is no obligation to pay ZUS contributions, no need for full accounting, and no registration in CEIDG. It is enough to keep simplified sales records and settle income in the annual PIT-36 return according to the tax scale (12% up to PLN 120,000, 32% above).

The limitation is not only the revenue limit but also the inability to issue VAT invoices (which excludes cooperation with many companies) and the lack of a NIP number as an entrepreneur. Unregistered activity works only as a temporary solution or a supplement to employment with small orders.

Business incubator

A business incubator is a solution that combines the advantages of running a business with the lack of formalities typical of your own company. The freelancer operates within the incubator entity, which issues invoices on their behalf, handles accounting, and settles taxes. The cost of using an incubator is approximately PLN 400 per month, covering full administrative support.

A key advantage of the incubator is no obligation to pay ZUS contributions (the freelancer is registered for insurance by the incubator on employment or contract terms). Additionally, within the incubator, it is possible to use preferential taxation — the flat-rate tax can be as low as 6% depending on the type of activity. The incubator also provides a registration address, which is important for people working from home.

The incubator is particularly attractive for people starting their freelancing adventure, foreigners (who may not have the right to register a JDG), and for specialists who don't want to deal with formalities. Your own company (JDG)

A sole proprietorship (JDG) is the classic form of running a business in Poland. Registration is free and done through the CEIDG portal. However, from the moment of registration, the entrepreneur becomes a ZUS contribution payer, which in 2026 means a cost of approximately PLN 1,600 per month (full ZUS) or approximately PLN 600 (Small ZUS Plus, if the discount applies).

JDG gives full freedom in running a business — the entrepreneur decides on the form of taxation (scale, 19% flat rate, lump sum), can deduct business costs, issue VAT invoices, and freely cooperate with domestic and foreign clients. From 2026, use of the National e-Invoice System (KSeF) becomes mandatory.

The disadvantage of JDG is the fixed ZUS cost regardless of revenue, the need for accounting (independently or through an accounting office for an additional fee), and full personal liability for business obligations. For freelancers with stable, high revenues (above PLN 8,000-10,000 per month), JDG is usually more profitable than an incubator.

Which model to choose?

The choice of model depends primarily on the level of revenue and stability of orders. If your monthly revenue does not exceed PLN 3,500 and you don't need VAT invoices, unregistered activity will be sufficient. It's a good starting point, allowing you to test the market without obligations.

A business incubator works well when your revenue is between PLN 3,000 and 10,000 per month, you need to issue invoices, but you don't want to pay ZUS and deal with accounting. It is also the only option for foreigners who cannot register a JDG. At a cost of approximately PLN 400 per month, the incubator offers comprehensive service and minimal formalities.

Your own company (JDG) becomes profitable with stable revenues above PLN 8,000-10,000 per month. Higher fixed costs (ZUS + accounting) are compensated by greater flexibility and tax optimization possibilities. Remember, however, that transitioning from an incubator to JDG is simple and can be done at any time — you don't have to make this decision right away.
