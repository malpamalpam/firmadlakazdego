How does the FDK incubator work for artisans?

Handcraft is a passion that is increasingly becoming a source of income. Original jewelry, ceramics, macrame, leather goods, candles, illustrations — you sell on Etsy, Allegro, at fairs or in brick-and-mortar shops. The problem arises when clients ask for an invoice and you do not have your own company. A sole proprietorship with fixed ZUS over PLN 1,825 per month is a huge cost, especially with irregular income.

The FDK incubator solves this problem. You join the Foundation and operate under its legal personality. FDK issues invoices to your customers — shops, galleries, boutiques, companies ordering gifts, fair organizers. You receive your fee minus only 6% PIT and the PLN 400 subscription. Zero ZUS, zero bureaucracy.

Original handcraft — unique items made by hand, with an individual creative character — qualifies as a copyrighted work. This makes it possible to apply 50% tax-deductible costs (KUP), giving an effective tax rate of just 6%. The same solution used by artists, graphic designers and other creators.

Why is FDK the ideal solution for artisans?

Handcraft as a creative work — 50% tax-deductible costs

Handmade, unique items of individual creative character may be recognized as copyrighted works. Original jewelry, artistic ceramics, illustrations, macrame, leather goods — if each piece is original and bears the hallmarks of individual creativity, it qualifies as a work. This gives the right to 50% KUP and an effective 6% tax rate.

Selling on Etsy, Allegro and in shops

Handcraft sold on platforms (Etsy, Allegro, Amazon Handmade) or in brick-and-mortar shops often requires an invoice. FDK issues VAT invoices to shops, galleries and boutiques, and for platform sales, we help settle the payments. You can sell professionally without your own sole proprietorship.

Wholesale orders and shop cooperation

Decor shops, art galleries, gift boutiques — these are natural B2B customers for artisans. They need VAT invoices and professional service. FDK issues invoices, prepares agreements and handles regular deliveries. You do not need your own company to professionally serve wholesale clients.

Handcraft workshops and team building

Ceramics, jewelry, macrame or calligraphy workshops — run according to an original program — are another source of income. Companies order handcraft workshops as team building, hotels offer them to guests, event agencies include them in programs. FDK invoices the organizers and handles framework agreements.

International sales

Polish handcraft is valued worldwide. Etsy is a global market, and customers from Europe, the US and Asia eagerly buy unique handmade items from Poland. FDK handles invoices in foreign currencies, applies the reverse charge procedure and advises on settling international sales. Build a global brand without a sole proprietorship.
