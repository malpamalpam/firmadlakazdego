How It Works

Working with the FDK incubator is simple and transparent. Learn the details of each stage.

01

Contact & Consultation

Reach out by phone, email, or contact form.

Contact us by phone, email, or through the form on our website. Our consultants are available in Polish, English, Ukrainian, and Russian.

During the first conversation, we'll discuss your business idea, answer questions about the incubator, and help you choose the best cooperation model. The consultation is free and non-binding.

You don't need a finished business plan — just an idea. We'll help you refine it.

02

Signing the Agreement

We sign a cooperation agreement. You receive a tax ID and can start invoicing.

After the consultation, we prepare the cooperation agreement. You can sign it in person at our office in Warsaw or remotely — via trusted signature (ePUAP) or qualified electronic signature.

The entire process usually takes 1-3 business days. After signing, you get access to the Foundation's tax ID (NIP) and REGON, an individual bank sub-account, and invoicing system access.

03

Starting Your Business

Start operating legally. Issue invoices, serve clients, grow your business.

From the moment you sign the agreement, you can immediately start your business. Issue invoices to your clients, receive payments to your individual bank account, and run your business under the Foundation's umbrella.

You don't need to register your own company, pay ZUS (applies to students under 26), or handle accounting on your own — we take care of all of that.

04

Ongoing Support

We provide accounting, legal, and business advisory throughout the cooperation.

Throughout the cooperation, we provide full accounting services, legal advisory (up to 5 hours per 12 months included in the subscription), tax and business consulting.

You deliver financial documents by the 10th of each month — our team handles the rest. You also have access to office space in Warsaw (up to 5 hours monthly).
