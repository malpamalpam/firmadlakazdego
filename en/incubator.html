AI tools usage policy

Why do we require an AI usage declaration?

The Firma Dla Kazdego Foundation requires every incubator participant to sign a declaration regarding the use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools as part of their assignments.

What does the declaration cover? The participant commits to transparently disclosing the extent of AI tool usage (e.g. ChatGPT, Midjourney, DALL-E, Copilot) when creating works under a contract for work with copyright transfer. The declaration covers, among others:

  • Whether and to what extent AI was used in the creation of the work
  • That the participant is the author of the final version of the work and bears responsibility for it
  • That the work does not infringe any third-party rights, including copyrights

Why does it matter? The FDK cooperation model is based on a contract for work with copyright transfer — which requires the work to have a creative nature and be the result of the author's effort. Using AI without disclosure may undermine the creative nature of the work, and consequently the legal basis for applying 50% tax-deductible costs. The declaration protects both the participant and the Foundation from tax and legal risks.

Regulatory basis: AI usage rules are part of the cooperation agreement and incubator regulations. Detailed provisions are included in the contract for work and the incubator regulations. [Final AI policy content requires substantive approval — to be confirmed]
